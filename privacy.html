Privacy Policy — Uploader

Last updated: 14 July 2026

Uploader ("the app") is a personal tool that lets its single operator publish their own short-form videos to their own TikTok account through TikTok's official Content Posting API. This policy explains what data the app touches and how it is handled.

Who this applies to

The app is operated privately by its owner for their own TikTok account. It does not offer accounts to, or process data on behalf of, any third party or member of the public.

Data the app accesses

  • TikTok authorization tokens. When you authorize the app, TikTok issues an access token and a refresh token. These are stored locally on the operator's own computer (in an environment file) and are used only to call the TikTok API on the operator's behalf.
  • Basic profile info (via the user.info.basic scope): your TikTok open ID / display name, used only to confirm which account is authorized.
  • Video files that the operator chooses to upload. These are the operator's own content and are sent directly to TikTok.

How data is used

Solely to authenticate with TikTok and to upload/post the operator's own videos. The app performs no analytics, profiling, or advertising.

Data sharing

Nothing is sold or shared with third parties. The only external service the app communicates with is TikTok's API, using the operator's own credentials.

Storage and retention

Tokens live only in a local configuration file on the operator's machine and can be revoked at any time from the TikTok account settings or by deleting the file. Video files remain on the operator's machine except for the copies uploaded to TikTok.

Your controls

Authorization can be withdrawn at any time in your TikTok account under Settings → Security & permissions → Manage app permissions, which immediately invalidates the app's tokens.
